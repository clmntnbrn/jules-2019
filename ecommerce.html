7th most valuable UK retail brand

Working with one of the UK’s largest retailers comes with tremendous responsibility and cache. They approached our agency hoping to evangelise their status in the beauty world as only 2.3% of their online business was beauty. The client wished to create awareness of their own brands, but also point out to consumers the company sold luxury items at competitive prices. My title began as that of Digital Editor. However, for this project, I became the Lead Digital Editor based on my pitch strategy and extensive experience in e-commerce.

Problems

  • Site didn’t evangelise their beauty brands on their main site nor on social media
  • The user journey wasn’t clear, so customers had too many hurdles to navigate to actually find the beauty hub
  • Images and product descriptions were outdated
  • There was no defined reason why consumers should buy from the client vs. competitors
  • A lack of the human-touch was missing making the buying experience lackluster

Solutions

Points to ensure the needs of the target audience was being met.

Competitor analysis gave me insight to show the client how they could be competitors in the beauty realm

I rewrote product descriptions in the appropriate TOV to be implemented by Copywriters for future content

I engaged influential vloggers and bloggers to evangelize products and to create conversations in the social sphere

Working with Google, I conceived the idea of embedding “Hangouts” on the client’s beauty hub. We decided to trial this over a one-month period.

Vloggers/bloggers were quickly booked by users eager to explore this new feature.

15 minute slots with these “specialists” allowed for a human touch and interactivity with the client as an approachable brand.

The bloggers/vloggers recommended products that were bespoke for the consumer…and not necessarily sold on the site.

This established trust and showed the brand genuinely cared about beauty needs—not just sales.

Results

During the test month that Beauty Hangouts was launched, the client had a conversion rate of 11.45%

The client found the click-through using the digital receipts sent by vloggers/bloggers after their session (which included hyperlinks directing users to discussed products) increased online sales by 38%

Vloggers/bloggers, though no longer paid, but continued to evangelize Tesco due to their positive experience with the brand. Again, another step to bridging the gap to their social media standing and humanizing their brand

After the launch, I did a second round of testing from consumers who had signed up for the Beauty Hangouts trial. The feedback was astonishing.

Each person I interviewed had changed their opinion about the client and he overall feedback was that the brand was a key player in the beauty sphere.
